**Discerning Your Purpose with God**  
====================================================================
=====================================  

Finding our purpose is a journey that brings both excitement and uncertainty. At its heart, purpose is not about achieving some grand destiny but about aligning our lives with God’s faithful and loving intentions for us. This guide invites you to begin uncovering how your everyday life can reflect the unique role God has for you in His larger story.  

## Scripture Foundation  

God’s Word offers a foundation for understanding purpose not as a isolated pursuit but as a journey of relationship and service.  

- **Jeremiah 29:11 (NIV)**: “For I know the plans I have for you,” declares the Lord, “plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.” This promise reminds us that God’s purposes are rooted in His faithfulness, not our performance.  
- **Matthew 6:33 (NIV)**: “But seek first His kingdom and His righteousness, and all these things will be given to you as well.” Jesus teaches that purpose begins with prioritizing God’s reign in our hearts.  
- **Colossians 3:23 (NIV)**: “Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord.” Our daily tasks, when offered to God, become acts of worship.  

These verses anchor purpose in God’s initiative, our alignment with His kingdom, and the sacredness of ordinary work.  

## What This Looks Like in Daily Life  

Discerning purpose isn’t about finding a hidden “answer” but about noticing how God is already working in your life. It might look like:  
- **Serving others** in small, faithful ways—listening to a friend, volunteering, or sharing your skills.  
- **Paying attention to joy and peace** in moments that align with your gifts and values.  
- **Praying for clarity** as you face decisions, big or small.  

> **Scripture Reflection:** When Paul writes, “Let us never grow weary of doing good” (Galatians 6:9, NIV), he reminds us that purpose is often lived out through consistent, loving action.  

## Questions for Reflection  

1. What activities make me feel most alive and connected to others?  
2. How might my current circumstances—work, relationships, challenges—be part of God’s preparation for my purpose?  
3. Where do I sense God gently nudging me to step forward in faith?  
4. What fears or doubts might be clouding my ability to discern His leading?  

## A Simple Practice This Week  

** Practice:** Spend 10 minutes reflecting on **Jeremiah 29:11**. Write down one hope or “future” God might be calling you toward. Share this with a trusted friend or community member for encouragement.  

**Next Step:** Begin a short daily prayer (e.g., “Lord, reveal Your purpose for me as I follow You today”) to cultivate attentiveness.  

## Prayer  

Heavenly Father,  
You know the depths of our hearts and the desires You’ve planted within us. Help us trust that Your purposes for us are good, even when the path feels unclear. Guide us to seek first Your kingdom, and may our lives reflect Your love in tangible ways. In Jesus’ name, Amen.  

## Key Takeaways  

- Purpose begins with God’s faithful plans, not our performance.  
- Align daily life with God’s kingdom through service and prayer.  
- Notice joy, peace, and gifts as signs of His leading.  
- Share your journey with trusted community for growth and support.
